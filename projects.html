Nisarg

An autonomous water surface cleaning robot designed to tackle water pollution in urban and industrial areas.

Problem We're Solving

Water pollution in densely populated areas where lakes, rivers, and coastal areas become dumping grounds for floating waste. Traditional cleanup methods are labor-intensive, expensive, and can't keep up with pollution rates.

Our Solution

Nisarg provides an efficient and scalable automated solution that continuously patrols and collects waste, ensuring water remains clean without constant human intervention while restoring water quality for aquatic life.

How It Works
  • Autonomous robot that floats on water surface.
  • Equipped with sensors to identify floating waste like plastic and organic debris.
  • Navigates across water using collection mechanisms to gather debris efficiently over large areas.
  • Utilizes solar power for sustainable operation.
  • Features remote monitoring and control capabilities.

Jal Nirikshak

A groundwater detection drone system that helps farmers locate optimal well drilling sites using advanced electromagnetic scanning technology.

Problem We're Solving

In rural and agricultural areas, farmers struggle to find exact groundwater locations, often relying on guesswork and outdated methods. This leads to wasted resources and inefficient use of scarce groundwater reserves.

Our Solution

Jal Nirikshak provides precise groundwater detection using electromagnetic waves, saving farmers time and money while promoting sustainable water management and effective resource utilization.

How It Works
  • Drone flies over fields scanning ground with electromagnetic waves.
  • Collects and analyzes data to map areas where water is most likely available.
  • Shares information with farmers to guide optimal well drilling locations via a mobile application.
  • Integrates GPS data for precise mapping.

Shuddhi

An automated desilting robot that removes accumulated sediment from water bodies to maintain proper depth and flow.

Problem We're Solving

Silt and sediment accumulation in lakes, rivers, and reservoirs reduces depth and obstructs water flow, leading to flood risks, habitat loss, and reduced water storage capacity. Traditional desilting is costly and labor-intensive.

Our Solution

Shuddhi automates the desilting process, making it efficient and less labor-intensive. It maintains water body depth and flow, reduces flood risks, and supports healthy aquatic ecosystems for community use.

How It Works
  • Modified version of Proton robot, upgraded for desilting operations.
  • Operates underwater using dredging tools and suction systems.
  • Lifts silt from bottom and transports to designated areas, restoring water flow and quality.
  • Equipped with depth sensors for precise operation.

Kalvari

An advanced Autonomous Underwater Vehicle (AUV) designed for complex underwater exploration, inspection, and object retrieval missions.

Problem We're Solving

Underwater exploration and tasks like structure inspection, object retrieval, and environmental surveys are complex, dangerous, and costly. Human divers face safety risks, especially in deep or hazardous environments.

Our Solution

Kalvari is an advanced AUV that performs underwater missions independently, showcasing TMRT's international-level technical expertise. It provides safe, efficient, and cost-effective underwater exploration while eliminating human risk.

How It Works
  • Equipped with HD cameras, sonar systems, and robotic arms.
  • Uses sensors and algorithms for autonomous underwater navigation and object detection.
  • Performs missions like equipment retrieval, pipeline inspection, and environmental data collection.
  • Features modular design for mission-specific configurations.

About TMRT

The Marine Robotics Team (TMRT) at K.J. Somaiya School of Engineering is dedicated to innovating in the field of autonomous underwater vehicles (AUVs) for exploration, research, and solving real-world marine challenges.
